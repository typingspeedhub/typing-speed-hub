How Typing Speed Is Measured

Most modern typing tests use a simple definition: 1 word = 5 characters, including spaces and punctuation. Your WPM is then calculated as:

WPM = (Total characters typed ÷ 5) ÷ Time in minutes

Many tests also show accuracy (%), which is just as important as speed. High speed with lots of mistakes doesn’t help much in real work.

Some tools also show CPM (Characters Per Minute). To roughly convert: WPM × 5 ≈ CPM.

What Counts as a “Good” WPM in 2024?

There isn’t a single universal cutoff, but most modern typing resources consider:

  • 40 WPM – functional everyday speed
  • 50–60 WPM – comfortable and efficient for most jobs
  • 70–80 WPM – fast typist, well above average
  • 100+ WPM – advanced / competitive typist

Typing-test data shows that the average “fast typist” sits around 70–75 WPM, while the global average is still roughly in the low 40s. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}

Speed vs. Accuracy: Which Matters More?

For everyday work, accuracy is more important than raw speed. Many keyboarding standards treat 95%+ accuracy as a healthy baseline. Below that, you may spend more time correcting typos than you save by typing quickly.

Simple benchmark to aim for

  • Goal 1: 40–50 WPM at 95–98% accuracy
  • Goal 2: 60+ WPM at 97–99% accuracy
  • Goal 3: Maintain speed for full 5–10 minute tests, not just 1 minute bursts

Many online tests show both WPM and accuracy. Focus on raising accuracy first; speed usually follows naturally once you stop making frequent mistakes.

How to Compare Your Typing Speed

To see where you stand:

  1. Take a few tests using the same language and time limit (for example, 1-minute English tests).
  2. Record your average WPM and accuracy over 3–5 attempts.
  3. Use the tables above to compare your numbers with typical age and job benchmarks.
  4. Set a realistic next goal (for example: from 35 → 45 WPM over the next month).

If you’re far below the averages, that’s okay — it just means you have a lot of room to grow. With a few weeks of structured practice you can often gain 10–20 WPM, especially if you’re still new to touch typing.

How to Improve Your Typing Speed in 2024

Knowing the statistics is useful, but the real progress comes from practice. Here are some evidence-based tips:

  • Learn proper finger placement and always return to the home row.
  • Practice without looking at the keyboard to build muscle memory.
  • Do short, focused sessions (5–10 minutes) instead of rare long marathons.
  • Mix speed tests with slower accuracy drills.
  • Track your WPM weekly to see steady improvement instead of obsessing over single tests.

Key Takeaways

  • The global average typing speed is roughly 36–42 WPM, with professionals often reaching 50–70 WPM.
  • Kids and teens naturally type slower than experienced adults, but can quickly improve with practice.
  • For most jobs, 60 WPM with good accuracy is more than enough.
  • Your goal speed depends on your age, role, and how much you type every day.
  • Consistency beats talent: a few minutes of focused practice each day will move you up the charts.

Wherever you’re starting from, you can steadily climb from “below average” to “fast typist” in a matter of months with the right habits — and a bit of patience.
