What is WPM and What’s a Good Typing Speed?

WPM stands for Words Per Minute, and it’s the standard unit used to measure typing speed. One “word” is typically calculated as 5 characters, including spaces and punctuation.

Why does WPM matter?

Your WPM reflects how efficiently you can type — especially important for students, office workers, writers, programmers, and anyone who uses a keyboard regularly.

What is a good WPM?

  • Beginner: 20–30 WPM
  • Average: 40–60 WPM
  • Proficient: 60–80 WPM
  • Advanced / Professional: 80–100+ WPM

Is speed or accuracy more important?

Accuracy always comes first. Speed will naturally increase as you practice typing correctly. If you type fast but constantly make mistakes, you’ll lose time correcting them — which defeats the purpose.

How to improve your WPM

  • Practice 5–10 minutes daily
  • Focus on accuracy before trying to speed up
  • Use tools that track your progress and make it fun (like the typing tests on this site)

With regular practice and the right mindset, most people can significantly increase their WPM in just a few weeks!
