  • ✅ Keep your fingers on the home row.
  • ✅ Don't look at the keyboard.
  • ✅ Practice daily for steady improvement.
  • ✅ Focus on accuracy before speed.
  • ✅ Use all 10 fingers while typing.

Common Typing Mistakes and How to Fix Them

Whether you're a beginner or someone trying to improve your typing speed, certain mistakes tend to appear over and over again. Recognizing these issues early on can help you become a faster, more accurate typist.

1. Looking at the Keyboard

❌ Why it’s a problem: This slows you down and makes it harder to develop true muscle memory.

✅ Fix it: Practice touch typing with home-row based lessons (ASDF – JKL;) and cover your hands if necessary. Use typing tools that provide on-screen keyboard guidance.

2. Incorrect Finger Placement

❌ Why it’s a problem: Using the wrong fingers for certain keys causes awkward hand movements and fatigue.

✅ Fix it: Learn and follow the standard finger-key mapping. Repetition and drills are key.

3. Skipping Punctuation and Capitalization

❌ Why it’s a problem: You might type fast, but skipping punctuation or caps makes your writing sloppy — especially in real-world scenarios.

✅ Fix it: Make sure to include punctuation and proper case when practicing. Use full-sentence typing tests that reflect real-world writing.

4. Rushing Through Practice

❌ Why it’s a problem: Typing fast with many errors isn’t real progress. Speed without accuracy leads to bad habits.

✅ Fix it: Slow down. Focus first on accuracy, then build up speed gradually. Use tools that measure WPM and accuracy together.

5. Inconsistent Practice

❌ Why it’s a problem: Typing is a muscle skill — and muscles need consistent repetition.

✅ Fix it: Create a daily habit: even 5–10 minutes a day is better than none. Consistency beats intensity.

Final Tip: Track Your Progress

Use a typing tool (like Typing Speed Hub!) that gives feedback over time. Celebrate improvements in WPM, but don’t ignore error rate.

✅ Summary

  • Avoid looking at the keyboard.
  • Use proper finger placement.
  • Include punctuation and capitalization.
  • Practice slowly and accurately.
  • Stay consistent with short daily sessions.
  • Track your WPM and accuracy over time.
