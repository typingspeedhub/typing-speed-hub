‚úÖ Before You Start: 3 Rules That Make This Plan Work

1) Accuracy comes first

Day 1 ‚Äî Home Row Fundamentals

The home row is the foundation of touch typing: ASDF for the left hand and JKL; for the right. Keep your fingers resting here after every movement.

  • Warm-up: type asdf jkl; slowly for 2 minutes.
  • Accuracy drill: type short words and focus on zero mistakes.
  • 1-minute test: take one baseline test on Typing Speed Test.

Goal: Learn proper finger positioning and reduce looking at the keyboard.

Day 2 ‚Äî Reach the Top and Bottom Rows

Expand from home row to the full keyboard. This is where most beginners lose accuracy because fingers ‚Äúhunt‚Äù for keys. Move slowly and return to home row every time.

  • Practice top row patterns (e.g., qwer, uiop).
  • Practice bottom row patterns (e.g., zxcv, bnm).
  • Do a 3-minute accuracy session: slow typing, perfect form.

Goal: Improve control and reduce finger confusion.

Now you‚Äôre training real typing: sentences, capitalization, and punctuation. This builds rhythm and reduces ‚Äústop-start‚Äù typing. If punctuation causes mistakes, slow down and focus on clean input.

Goal: Confirm progress and choose a next-step routine.

FAQ: 7-Day Typing Plan

Most people see progress with 10‚Äì20 minutes per day. Short, consistent sessions work better than long, irregular practice.

Accuracy first. Aim for 95%+ accuracy during most practice. Once accuracy is stable, your WPM rises naturally.

What results can I expect after 7 days?

Many beginners reduce mistakes quickly and often improve by about 3‚Äì10 WPM. Intermediate users typically see steadier accuracy and a smaller WPM increase with better rhythm.
