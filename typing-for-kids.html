Typing for Kids: How to Get Started

In today's digital world, typing is just as essential for kids as learning to read and write. Whether it's for school assignments, online learning, or future job readiness, early exposure to proper typing techniques can set children up for success.

Need a warm-up idea? Try our 10-Minute Typing Drill for kids!

Why Should Kids Learn to Type Early?

Touch typing builds confidence, digital literacy, and long-term skills. When children learn to type early, they develop:

  • Better focus and organization
  • Faster homework and test completion
  • Improved spelling and writing skills
  • More confidence using computers

1. Choose Kid-Friendly Typing Tools

Start with platforms designed for children. Look for games that are:

  • Colorful, interactive, and game-based
  • Ad-free and distraction-free
  • Progressive in difficulty

Top picks include TypingClub for Kids, BBC Dance Mat Typing, and NitroType for older learners.

2. Start With the Basics: Home Row and Finger Placement

Before starting games, teach hand position:

  • Home row keys: A–S–D–F (left) and J–K–L–; (right)
  • Thumbs on the spacebar
  • F and J keys usually have bumps to guide fingers

3. Keep Practice Sessions Short and Fun

Stick to 10–15 minutes a day, 3–5 days a week. Use:

  • Typing games with rewards
  • Progress charts or stickers
  • Typing "challenges" to boost motivation

4. Focus on Accuracy First, Speed Later

Encourage slow, error-free typing. Build good habits by:

  • Using all 10 fingers
  • Not looking at the keyboard
  • Rewarding mistake-free sentences

5. Make Typing Part of Everyday Life

Typing becomes natural with real-world use:

  • Write emails to friends or family
  • Journal digitally
  • Do homework on a computer

6. Monitor Progress with Typing Tests

Use beginner-friendly typing tests to track growth. Suggested WPM goals:

  • Age 6–8: 5–15 WPM
  • Age 9–12: 20–35 WPM
  • Teens: 40+ WPM

Final Thoughts

Typing doesn’t have to be boring. With the right tools and support, kids can develop this lifelong skill with joy and confidence.
